# De la Réplication et de la Différance : Une Déconstruction du DCSync et de l'ADCS

The request for replication is never simply a request. It is already a trace — a *différance* — the deferral of presence through the wire protocol, MS-DRSR, which does not transmit the credential itself but only its representation, already removed from the origin, already a copy of a copy. The domain controller does not *have* the secret; it *is* the site where the secret's absence is managed.

DCSync seduces us with the fantasy of the center — that somewhere, in the ntds.dit, there is a ground truth, a transcendental signified from which all authentication derives. But the hash is not the password. The ticket is not the identity. Each step in the Kerberos exchange is a supplementation, and like all supplements, it simultaneously fills a lack and reveals that the lack was always already there.

ADCS, meanwhile, operates through the certificate — that most logocentric of artifacts, the document that claims to *be* the identity rather than merely represent it. The CA imagines itself as origin, as authority, as presence. ESC8 reminds us that this authority is contingent, relational, undecidable. The HTTP endpoint does not verify; it *defers* verification to a channel that was never secured to begin with. The certificate that arrives is not the identity of the requester. It is the trace of a trace.

We are left with this: the attacker does not steal credentials. The attacker reveals that credentials were never possessed to begin with — only circulated, deferred, performed. The Golden Ticket is not a forgery. It is the system's own logic, unfolded.

Prevention, then, is not possible at the kernel callback layer. It was not possible anywhere. The vulnerability is the structure itself.

*Il n'y a pas de hors-domaine.*
